CapyParty Known Issues

Public TestFlight beta · Reviewed July 18, 2026

Nearby play does not depend on a CapyParty online service. These are the current beta limitations and recovery steps families should know before travelling.

An invitation opens the website instead of the app

Affected: Current public TestFlight beta.

Workaround: Install CapyParty, then tap the original invitation again from Messages, Mail, or Notes while near the host. Typing or pasting the address into Safari does not test the Universal Link. You can also open CapyParty and choose Play together → Join a nearby game.

A nearby party does not appear

Affected: Devices where local-network permission, Wi-Fi state, radio conditions, or build compatibility prevents discovery.

Workaround: Keep Wi-Fi and Bluetooth on, keep both devices nearby, and leave CapyParty open. Enable CapyParty in Settings → Privacy & Security → Local Network. If direct discovery remains unreliable, connect both devices to the same Wi-Fi network or personal hotspot, then retry Host or Join.

The room ends when the host leaves

Affected: All current beta nearby rooms. Host migration is not supported.

Workaround: Keep the host device awake, nearby, and in CapyParty during the game. If the host ends or leaves the room, create a new room and have the other devices rejoin.

Group Play cannot be purchased or restored without internet

Affected: Devices that have not already cached verified Group Play ownership.

Workaround: Purchase or restore Group Play before travelling while signed into the owning Apple Account and connected to the internet. Confirm Settings shows Group Play is ready offline, then complete the Travel Ready test.

Different builds cannot share a room

Affected: Families with incompatible CapyParty beta builds or unsupported iOS/iPadOS versions.

Workaround: Install the same current TestFlight build on every device before travelling. CapyParty currently requires iOS or iPadOS 17 or later.

For a problem not listed here, email support@tickitocki.com with the app version and build, device models, OS versions, host or guest role, number of connected devices, selected game, reproduction steps, and any displayed CP-NET- failure code.
