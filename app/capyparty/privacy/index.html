CapyParty Privacy Policy

Effective July 18, 2026

CapyParty has no accounts, advertisements, third-party analytics, cross-app tracking, or developer-operated game server. Nearby games run directly between devices you choose to connect.

Information CapyParty does not collect

CapyParty does not collect contacts, precise location, advertising identifiers, payment-card information, or personal profiles. It does not sell personal information and does not track activity across other companies’ apps or websites.

Information stored on your device

CapyParty stores only information needed to operate the app:

  • preferences and a cached Group Play ownership status;
  • a private device key used to authenticate paired devices;
  • trusted nearby-device public keys and the display names those devices advertise;
  • session or solo-game state where needed for play and reconnection;
  • a bounded connection log and up to ten locally retained Apple MetricKit diagnostic or performance payloads.

Preferences and trust records remain until reset or uninstall. Session data is normally discarded when the session ends. The oldest diagnostic payload is deleted when the local ten-payload limit is exceeded.

Nearby multiplayer

When you host or join a nearby party, CapyParty uses Apple local-network APIs to discover and communicate with devices on the nearby peer-to-peer network. Connected devices exchange the information necessary to authenticate the connection and run the selected game, including an advertised display name, public device identity, room state, and game actions. This traffic is not routed through a CapyParty developer server.

Diagnostics and support

Connection logs use bounded, fixed-format entries and discard peer names, network endpoints, device fingerprints, and raw system-error descriptions before storage. Diagnostic and MetricKit files stay on your device unless you explicitly export and send them for support. You control the recipient through the system share sheet.

Purchases

Apple processes TestFlight distribution and App Store purchases. CapyParty receives verified entitlement status from StoreKit and caches ownership for offline use. Apple’s handling of purchase and account information is governed by Apple’s privacy policy.

Children

CapyParty is designed for shared family play and does not knowingly collect personal information from children. Families should avoid placing a child’s full name or other identifying information in a nearby-device display name or support message.

Changes

If CapyParty adds developer-operated analytics, accounts, online services, or another material data practice, this policy and the App Store privacy disclosure will be updated before that collection begins.

Contact

Privacy questions can be sent to support@tickitocki.com.
